Recurve bow

The main components include the bow body of the grip, the upper and lower arms, and

the bow string. The materials are composed of low-grade wood, aluminum alloy, composite

and carbon fiber. It is called a "recurved" bow because the two bow arms bend forward

when there is no force, and the bow arms must be bent backward and fixed by the bowstring.

Archery is to rely on the tension of the two arms to hold the arrow Shoot out instead of

relying on the elasticity of the bowstring.
